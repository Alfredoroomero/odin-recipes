Spanish Croqueta (Cocido)

Description

Spanish croquettes are a classic tapa made with the leftover meat from cocido, a traditional stew. The meat is finely chopped, mixed with a creamy béchamel sauce, coated in breadcrumbs, and fried until golden. Crispy on the outside and soft inside, these croquettes are a beloved comfort food across Spain.

Ingredients

  • 200 g leftover cocido meat (chicken, beef, or pork)
  • 50 g butter
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 100 g (¾ cup) all-purpose flour
  • 750 ml (3 cups) whole milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • Breadcrumbs (for coating)
  • Olive oil (for frying)

Steps

  1. Finely chop the leftover cocido meat.
  2. In a pan, melt butter with olive oil. Add flour and cook for 2 minutes to make a roux.
  3. Gradually whisk in milk to form a thick béchamel sauce.
  4. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg. Stir in the chopped meat.
  5. Cook for 5/10 minutes until thick. Transfer to a tray, cover, and chill for at least 2 hours.
  6. Shape the mixture into small cylinders or balls.
  7. Dip each croquette in beaten egg, then coat with breadcrumbs.
  8. Heat olive oil in a frying pan and fry croquettes until golden brown on all sides.
  9. Drain on paper towels and serve hot.
